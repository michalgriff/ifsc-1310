Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive Enhancement is a relatively new concept when it comes to technology. While commonplace now, Progressive Enhancement has come a long way. Its growth has replaced graceful degradation which focuses on the browser’s ability to at least be passable as functioning.

Progressive Enhancement focuses on the content in a site. Without the content, a website is essentially nothing. The analogy of a Peanut M&M is used to explain the progressive enhancement approach: content as the peanut, CSS as the chocolate coating, and JavaScript as the hard candy shell. Each layer caters to different user capabilities, ensuring a basic experience for all while enhancing it for those with more advanced browsers. Progressive enhancement is touted for its simplicity and adherence to web standards.
